## Introduction

The Zae Project is an ambitious research initiative exploring consciousness substrate transfer through a novel integration of:
- **Arkspace-core**: LEO neuromorphic satellite constellation  
- **Brain-emulation**: Corpus callosum brain-computer interface  
- **Neutral-consciousness-engine**: Spiking neural network software platform

This bibliography represents the intellectual foundations from which the Zae Project draws inspiration, technical approaches, and theoretical frameworks.

## Consciousness Studies

### Key Philosophers & Theorists

#### David Chalmers
- **Institution**: New York University, Australian National University
- **Key Contributions**: Formulated the "hard problem of consciousness" (1995)
- **Major Works**:
  - *The Conscious Mind: In Search of a Fundamental Theory* (1996)
  - "Facing Up to the Problem of Consciousness" (1995)
  - "The Meta-Problem of Consciousness" (2018)
- **Relevance to Zae**: Philosophical framework for understanding substrate-independent consciousness

#### Thomas Nagel
- **Institution**: New York University
- **Key Contributions**: Subjective character of experience
- **Major Works**:
  - "What Is It Like to Be a Bat?" (1974) - Foundational paper on qualia
  - *The View from Nowhere* (1986)
  - *Mind and Cosmos* (2012)
- **Relevance to Zae**: Questions about first-person perspective in consciousness transfer

#### Daniel Dennett
- **Institution**: Tufts University
- **Key Contributions**: Multiple drafts model of consciousness, functionalism
- **Major Works**:
  - *Consciousness Explained* (1991)
  - *From Bacteria to Bach and Back* (2017)
  - "Quining Qualia" (1988)
- **Relevance to Zae**: Functionalist account compatible with substrate transfer

#### John Searle
- **Institution**: University of California, Berkeley
- **Key Contributions**: Chinese Room argument, biological naturalism
- **Major Works**:
  - "Minds, Brains, and Programs" (1980)
  - *The Rediscovery of the Mind* (1992)
  - *The Mystery of Consciousness* (1997)
- **Relevance to Zae**: Challenges to computational theory of mind

### Neuroscientists

#### Christof Koch
- **Institution**: Allen Institute for Brain Science, Caltech (former)
- **Key Contributions**: Neural correlates of consciousness, integrated information theory
- **Major Works**:
  - *The Quest for Consciousness* (2004)
  - *Consciousness: Confessions of a Romantic Reductionist* (2012)
  - With Francis Crick: "Towards a Neurobiological Theory of Consciousness" (1990)
- **Relevance to Zae**: NCCs provide empirical grounding for consciousness detection/transfer

#### Giulio Tononi
- **Institution**: University of Wisconsin-Madison
- **Key Contributions**: Integrated Information Theory (IIT)
- **Major Works**:
  - "An Information Integration Theory of Consciousness" (2004)
  - *Phi: A Voyage from the Brain to the Soul* (2012)
  - "Consciousness as Integrated Information" (2008)
- **Relevance to Zae**: IIT provides quantitative framework for measuring consciousness (Φ)

#### Rodolfo Llinás
- **Institution**: New York University School of Medicine
- **Key Contributions**: Thalamocortical dialogue theory
- **Major Works**:
  - *I of the Vortex: From Neurons to Self* (2001)
  - "Thalamocortical dysrhythmia: A neurological and neuropsychiatric syndrome" (1999)
- **Relevance to Zae**: Neural oscillations and binding problem solutions

#### Stanislas Dehaene
- **Institution**: Collège de France
- **Key Contributions**: Global workspace theory, neural signatures of consciousness
- **Major Works**:
  - *Consciousness and the Brain* (2014)
  - "Toward a computational theory of conscious processing" (2011)
  - "What is consciousness, and could machines have it?" (2017)
- **Relevance to Zae**: Computational models of consciousness mechanisms

### Foundational Papers

1. **Nagel, T.** (1974). "What is it like to be a bat?" *The Philosophical Review*, 83(4), 435-450.
2. **Chalmers, D. J.** (1995). "Facing up to the problem of consciousness." *Journal of Consciousness Studies*, 2(3), 200-219.
3. **Crick, F., & Koch, C.** (1990). "Towards a neurobiological theory of consciousness." *Seminars in the Neurosciences*, 2, 263-275.
4. **Tononi, G.** (2004). "An information integration theory of consciousness." *BMC Neuroscience*, 5(1), 42.
5. **Dehaene, S., & Changeux, J. P.** (2011). "Experimental and theoretical approaches to conscious processing." *Neuron*, 70(2), 200-227.

## Neuromorphic Computing & Spiking Neural Networks

### Pioneers

#### Carver Mead
- **Institution**: Caltech
- **Key Contributions**: Coined "neuromorphic engineering", analog VLSI
- **Major Works**:
  - "Neuromorphic electronic systems" (1990) *Proceedings of the IEEE*
  - *Analog VLSI and Neural Systems* (1989)
- **Relevance to Zae**: Foundational principles for neuromorphic hardware design

#### Kwabena Boahen
- **Institution**: Stanford University
- **Key Contributions**: Neurogrid system, low-power neuromorphic computing
- **Major Works**:
  - "Neurogrid: A Mixed-Analog-Digital Multichip System for Large-Scale Neural Simulations" (2014)
  - "Point-to-point connectivity between neuromorphic chips using address events" (2000)
- **Relevance to Zae**: Energy-efficient neural processing architectures

#### Karlheinz Meier
- **Institution**: University of Heidelberg
- **Key Contributions**: BrainScaleS project, analog neuromorphic systems
- **Major Works**:
  - "The BrainScaleS Project" (2013)
  - "Accelerated physical emulation of spiking neural networks" (2008)
- **Relevance to Zae**: Accelerated neural simulation for rapid prototyping

#### Giacomo Indiveri
- **Institution**: University of Zurich
- **Key Contributions**: Neuromorphic circuits, event-based processing
- **Major Works**:
  - "Neuromorphic silicon neuron circuits" (2011)
  - "A VLSI array of low-power spiking neurons and bistable synapses with spike-timing dependent plasticity" (2006)
- **Relevance to Zae**: Low-power spike-based computation

### Key Researchers (Current)

#### Chris Eliasmith
- **Institution**: University of Waterloo
- **Key Contributions**: Neural Engineering Framework, Spaun brain model
- **Major Works**:
  - *How to Build a Brain* (2013)
  - "A large-scale model of the functioning brain" (2012) - *Science*
  - *Neural Engineering* (2003) with Charles H. Anderson
- **Relevance to Zae**: Semantic Pointer Architecture for cognitive modeling

#### Tobi Delbruck
- **Institution**: University of Zurich, ETH Zurich
- **Key Contributions**: Neuromorphic vision sensors, event-based computing
- **Major Works**:
  - "Activity-driven, event-based vision sensors" (2010)
  - "A 128×128 120 dB 15 μs Latency Asynchronous Temporal Contrast Vision Sensor" (2008)
